FAQ

Is this a list of accomplishments?

No. The public title avoids that framing because “accomplishments” can imply approval. The project ranks consequences and scale of impact.

What was Trump’s most consequential policy?

According to this ranking methodology, the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act ranks first because it affected most U.S. taxpayers and businesses while also changing federal revenue, corporate taxation, household tax liability, investment incentives, and deficit projections.

What were Trump’s biggest accomplishments?

This site does not label actions as accomplishments or failures. However, actions often cited positively by supporters include the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, Supreme Court appointments, Operation Warp Speed, the Abraham Accords, USMCA, the First Step Act, and parts of the administration’s immigration, energy, and trade agenda.

What were Trump’s biggest failures or most criticized actions?

The site does not label actions as failures, but actions frequently criticized by opponents include family-separation-related immigration policies, tariffs, COVID response decisions, WHO withdrawal, USAID restructuring, environmental rollbacks, and efforts affecting federal agencies, public health, and international institutions.

What did Trump actually do?

The rankings are intended to answer that question in a concrete way. Full entries describe what changed, who was affected, how supporters interpret the action, how critics interpret it, and why it ranks where it does.

Why are #26–100 stubs?

This is a public beta intended for feedback. The first 25 entries show the intended final editorial style, while the remaining entries preserve the full ranking order for review.
